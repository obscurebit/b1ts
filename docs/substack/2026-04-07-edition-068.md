### The Sock Cipher

**INTERVIEW TRANSCRIPT: SUBJECT [REDACTED], DAY 3**  
**TIME: 14:47. LOCATION: UNDERGROUND FACILITY "BUNKER L," LEVEL 4**  

**INTERVIEWER:** Subject, you’ve had 48 hours to review the file. Let’s start with the sock.  

**SUBJECT:** [LAUGHTER] You’re still fixated on the sock?  

**INTERVIEWER:** The artifact—recovered from Vault 7 on [REDACTED]—is priority. Describe its significance.  

**SUBJECT:** It’s a woolen sock. Argyle pattern. Smelled like mothballs and regret.  

**INTERVIEWER:** Timestamp: 1963-11-14. Your team logged 217 attempts to decrypt the message embedded in—  

**SUBJECT:** —in the stitching. Yes. We called it “The Knitter’s Curse.”  

**[REDACTED SECTION: 3 LINES]**  

**INTERVIEWER:** Colleague Renzo Aomine argued the cipher was “unbreakable by design.” Confirm.  

**SUBJECT:** Renzo thought everything was unbreakable by design. Including the coffee machine.  

**INTERVIEWER:** Yet you prioritized the sock over the [REDACTED] intercepts. Why?  

**SUBJECT:** Because the Director’s wife knitted it. And because [PAUSE] because it smelled like home. People hide truths in comfort.  

**[REDACTED SECTION: 1 LINE]**  

**INTERVIEWER:** Timeline discrepancy. Your report claims the sock was found in a subway tunnel beneath [REDACTED]. Later, you stated it was “left on the bunker’s copy machine.”  

**SUBJECT:** Maybe it moved. Or maybe I lied to see if you’d notice.  

**INTERVIEWER:** The message—  

**SUBJECT:** —wasn’t a message. It was a recipe. For goulash.  

**[REDACTED SECTION: 2 LINES]**  

**INTERVIEWER:** Subject, this isn’t a joke. The cipher’s decryption window closes in 12 hours. If the sequence—  

**SUBJECT:** There is no sequence. Just yarn and a handful of letters that spell “sour cream.” Check the bunker’s kitchen. Maybe the goulash is ready.  

**[STATIC HUM. DOOR CREAKS.]**  

**INTERVIEWER:** [WHISPERS] Why the subway?  

**SUBJECT:** Because that’s where Renzo met his contact. A man in a trench coat, shouting into a payphone about “the socks that bind us.” Renzo threw the sock into the tracks. Said it was “too heavy to carry.”  

**INTERVIEWER:** Heavy with what?  

**SUBJECT:** Guilt? Wool? [LEANS FORWARD] Or maybe the weight of a secret that’s not meant to surface.  

**[ALARM SOUNDS. REDACTED VOICES OVER INTERCOM.]**  

**INTERVIEWER:** Final question. If the cipher is a recipe, what does it decrypt to?  

**SUBJECT:** To a meal no one eats alone.  

**[TRANSCRIPT TERMINATED. FILE STATUS: PENDING DECLASSIFICATION.]**  

**APPENDED NOTE (HANDWRITTEN):** Check Vault 7’s kitchen. Bring sour cream.
